 What You Should Do (The Do’s)Privacy Control: 
	Set your personal accounts to private to maintain control over who can view your content.
	Professionalism: Use a high-quality, professional headshot and a concise bio on career-oriented platforms 			like LinkedIn and GitHub.
	Mindful Posting: Practice the "ten-second rule" by pausing before you hit post to ensure your content is 			respectful and appropriate.
	Academic Integrity: Always credit the original creators or sources when sharing articles, images, or 				snippets of code.
	Account Security: Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all social media accounts to prevent 				unauthorized access.
 What You Should Avoid (The Don’ts)Information Leakage:
	Never post sensitive personal data, such as your home address, phone number, or government ID details.
	Negative Behavior: Avoid engaging in or encouraging cyberbullying, harassment, or inflammatory arguments in 			comment sections.
	Red Flags: Do not post photos or comments that depict illegal activities or any behavior that could be seen 			as unprofessional by future employers.
	Public Venting: Refrain from complaining about professors, your institution, or peers on public social media 			forums.
	Suspicious Links: Never click on "clickbait" or suspicious links sent via direct messages, as these are 			common entry points for phishing attacks.
